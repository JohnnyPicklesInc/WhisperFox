🦊 WhisperFox for Slack

Stop pasting passwords into channels where they live forever. /whisperfox turns a secret into a link that self-destructs — and by default the secret is encrypted in your browser, so it never reaches Slack or our servers.

Add to Slack

Two ways to use it — pick your trade-off

  1. /whisperfox — zero-knowledge (recommended) Posts a private button only you can see. Click it to open the WhisperFox compose page, type your secret there, and it's encrypted on your device. Slack and our servers never see the text — just the finished link, which you can post back to the channel with one click.
  2. /whisperfox quick <secret> — convenient, server-side Encrypts the secret on our server and drops a one-time link straight into the channel (it burns after the first read and expires in 15 minutes). This is faster, but honestly: the text travels through Slack and our Worker in the clear for that moment, unlike the default flow. Use it for low-stakes secrets; use the button for anything sensitive.

Either way, the secret never lands in your channel's permanent scrollback — the link does, and it stops working on its own. Add phrase:<word> to quick (or set a phrase in the browser) to require a separately-shared word to open it.

What we store

For the default flow: nothing about your secret — it's encrypted in your browser and sealed inside the link, exactly like the core WhisperFox product. For quick mode: the server briefly handles the plaintext to encrypt it, then stores nothing but the same content-free key material every message uses, which self-erases within about a day. We keep a minimal "installed" marker per workspace — no message data.
